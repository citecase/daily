Joint Director (Rayalaseema), ACB, A.P. v. Dayam Peda Ranga Rao

CrPC - Is It Necessary That Specific Place Has To Be Declared As A Police Station?

Code of Criminal Procedure 1973 - Section 2(s): There need not be a specific place to be declared as a police station, as even a post being held by a police officer would constitute a police station- The definition clause is both exhaustive and inclusive. It is exhaustive to mean, any post or any place, while it includes any local area specified by the State Government.

Section 2(o): This is an inclusive definition, which refers to a police officer at the station house, placed next in rank to the officer in charge, and is above the rank of constable, unless the State Government otherwise so directs. (Para 4)

Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014 - Section 2(f): The term ‘law’ includes an order or other instrument having the force of law, and therefore, brings within its ambit a notification or a circular issued by a competent authority.

Section 100-102: Section 100 is a transitional provision, making the application of the existing law to the two States, conscious enough not to create any legal vacuum. The State of Andhra Pradesh continues to be the same State; a new State has been created by merely carving out territories. The legal fiction ensures existing laws continue in force unless altered. (Para 8-10, 26)

Md Imran @ D.C. Guddu v. State of Jharkhand

S.319 CrPC - Bail - Tests

Code of Criminal Procedure 1973 - Section 319: When a person is added as an accused and arrested, the relevant consideration for bail should be strong and cogent evidence than mere probability of complicity. The test is more than a prima facie case as exercised at the time of framing of charge, but short of satisfaction that evidence would lead to conviction if unrebutted. The Court must be satisfied of complicity at a threshold much higher than that required for framing charges. (Para 14)

Specific Performance

Sentence Suspension Criteria

Sukhchain v. State of Madhya Pradesh

CrPC/BNSS - How To Consider Sentence Suspension Plea Of A Convict Sentenced To Fixed Period?

Code of Criminal Procedure 1973 - Section 389: When a convicted person is sentenced to a fixed period and files an appeal, suspension of sentence should be considered by the Appellate Court liberally unless there are exceptional circumstances. This differs from life imprisonment sentences.

Stamp Act - Bond vs MoU

Sri Lakshmi Balaji Enterprises & Anr. v. Angeethis Restaurant

Indian Stamp Act - Section 2(5) - Bond: An instrument where a person puts himself under an obligation to pay money on condition that the obligation shall be void if a specific act is performed. If the executant can be sued for that sum only on the strength of the instrument, it is a bond.

Memorandum of Understanding (MOU): MOU cannot be treated as Bond when signed by both parties and not by one party binding himself to pay. If the obligation to pay is not dependent on a condition and the document is signed by both parties making it an agreement, it does not attract Section 2(5)(a) to (c).
